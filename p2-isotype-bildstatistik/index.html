Bildstatistik in Place

Türkenbefreiungsfeier of the Heimatschutz · Schönbrunn gardens · 14 May 1933

Narrated in Otto Neurath's pictorial idiom — the Wiener Methode der Bildstatistik that Red Vienna built to make society legible, and that the Austrofascists dismantled when Neurath fled in 1934. Here it is turned back on the regime's own rally. The argument is carried by the apparatus of amplification, not by crowd size: one icon is one verified thing.

Verified  documented count (Ganahl 2024) Estimate  approximate, marked as such Gap  undocumented — shown empty, never filled Different event  not 14 May — shown for contrast

The reading this board proposes (yours to accept or revise): the rally's own voice was acoustically modest and under-documented — six microphones, a presumed central speaker pair, forty-five minutes of radio — while the machinery of total amplification (forty-eight distributed loudspeakers for three hundred thousand) was built months later, for the Church. The spectacle was manufactured incrementally. Attendance on 14 May is left blank on purpose: counting it would re-stage the grandeur the rally was engineered to project.
