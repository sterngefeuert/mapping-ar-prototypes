Mapping Austrofascism, in place

WebXR research prototypes for situated, in-place storytelling at a contested site

About these prototypes. Research-through-design sketches — not products. They explore carrying the multi-perspective cartography of Campus Medius — which mediates the Austrofascist “Turks Deliverance Celebration” (Türkenbefreiungsfeier) of 14 May 1933 in the gardens of Schönbrunn Palace, Vienna — off the screen and onto the historical ground. Each sketch occupies one position in a design space and is kept “legible enough to occupy its cell,” not polished.
On the data. Every quantity shown is a verified count of the event’s apparatus, not crowd-size triumphalism. Attendance on 14 May is a deliberate blank, not invented. The distributed loudspeakers and large crowd belong to a different event (the September 1933 Catholic Mass) and appear only when explicitly labelled.

Try in AR Android + Chrome (ARCore)

Real WebXR. Open on an Android phone in Chrome, tap Start AR, point at the ground, and tap to place content at 1:1. iOS Safari does not support WebXR AR, and these are not geo-pinned to the real site (tap-to-place on a detected surface).
