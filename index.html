Mapping Austrofascism, in place

Research prototypes for situated, in-place storytelling at a contested site

About these prototypes. These are research-through-design sketches, not products and not finished applications. They explore how the multi-perspective cartography of the Campus Medius platform — which mediates the Austrofascist “Turks Deliverance Celebration” (Türkenbefreiungsfeier) of 14 May 1933 in the gardens of Schönbrunn Palace, Vienna — might be carried off the screen and onto the historical ground itself. Each sketch deliberately occupies one position in a design space and is kept “legible enough to occupy its cell,” not polished.
On the data. Every quantity shown is a verified count of the event’s apparatus (microphones, recording and broadcast machinery), not crowd-size triumphalism. Attendance on 14 May is left a deliberate blank rather than invented. The distributed loudspeakers and large crowd belong to a different event — the September 1933 Catholic Mass — and are shown only when explicitly labelled as such. No figures are fabricated to fill a chart.
